Lemon Bars

Description

I have used this recipe time and time again to make delicious lemon bars for my girlfriend. With a little finesse and trial and error, you can figure out the best proportion of sugar and lemon juice to fit your pallet. I always go a little overboard with the lemon zest and juice, and my girlfriend absolutely loves it.

Debatably the most important part of this recipe is the crust. If you bake it just right, you will get a delicious and moist base for your lemon top. If not, it will be very crumbly and dry. Make sure to watch its color and to not overbake it.

Ingredients

Crust

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup white sugar

Filling

  • 1 1/2 cups white sugar
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 4 eggs
  • 2 lemons, juiced
  • 2 tablespoons lemon zest

Steps

  1. Preheat the over to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C)
  2. Crust: Blend 2 cups flour, softened butter, and 1/2 cup sugar in medium bowl until well combined; press into the bottom of an ungreased 9x13-inch pan.
  3. Bake in a preheated oven until firm and golden, about 15 minutes. Meanwhile, make the filling by whisking remaining 1 1/2 cups sugar and 1/4 cup flour in a meidum bowl. Whisk in eggs, then lemon juice until smooth; pour filling over the baked crust.
  4. Bake in the preheated oven for 20 minutes. Set the pan aside to cool completely; the bars will firm up as they cool. Then cooled, cut into uniform squares.
