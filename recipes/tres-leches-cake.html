Tres Leches Cake

Description

The first time I ever ate tres leches cake was in Spanish class in middle school. It took me so long to eat that my napking was soaked through and my desk was wet. I remember it being one of the tastiest things I ever had, as well as one of the most unique. I had never had a desert that was so moist and sweet, while not being heavy.

Now, whenever my sister's boyfiredn and I are in the same place for more than a day, he asks me to make it for him. It is his favorite desert, and I happily oblige.

Ingredients

Cake

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 5 large eggs, seperated
  • 1 cup granulated sugar, divided
  • 1/3 cup whole milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Milk Mixture

  • 12 ounce can evaporated milk
  • 14 ounce can sweetened condensed milk
  • 1/4 cup whole milk

Whipped Topping

  • 1 pint heavy whipping cream
  • 3 tablespoons powdered sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • ground cinnamon, for topping

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C)
  2. In a medium bowl combine flour, baking powder, and salt. Seperate the eggs into two other mixing bowls.
  3. Add 3/4 cup suagr to the bowl with the egg yolks and mix on high speed until yolks are pale yellow. Add 1/3 cup milk and vanilla and stir to combine. Pour the egg yolk mixture over the flour mixture and stir gently just until combined (don't over-mix)
  4. Use electric beaters to beat the egg whites on high speed. As they begin to whip into stiff peaks, gradually mix in the remaining 1/4 cup of sugar. Fold the stiffly beaten egg whites into the batter gently, scraping the bottom and sides of the bowl, until combined.
  5. Pour batter into un-greased pan and smooth it into an even layer. Bake for 25 to 35 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted in the center of the cake comes out clean. Remove from the oven and allow cake to cool completely.
  6. Combine evaporated milk, sweetened condensed milk, and whole milk in small bowl. Once the cake has cooled use a fork to poke holes all ocer the top of the cake.
  7. Slowly pour the milk mixture over the top of the cake, making sure to pour near the edges and all around. Refrigerate the cake for at least 1 hour or overnight, to allow it to soak up the milk.
  8. In the meantime, whip the heavy cream, sugar, and vanilla until stiff peaks form. Smooth over the top of the cake. Sprinkle cinnamon on top. Serve with fresh sliced strawberries, if desired. Enjoy!
